## ![STUDPRINT](Gallery/frontpage.png)

**A standard I3 style bedslinger...but with a twist! The frame, motion system, and extruder are made out of 91%[^1] classic LEGO bricks.**

## About

This project started in response to a Hackclub YSWS called [Infill](https://infill.hackclub.com). The pitch was to make a 3D printer under $350 with a twist. Having grown up with LEGO, I decided to just make my entire printer out of it!

To make this printer one-of-a-kind, I decided to take it a step further and not only build it out of LEGO, but also use only classical LEGO bricks[^2] wherever I could. As you might expect, some parts of it — notably the parts that make the printer move and mounts for the steppers, electronics, etc. — cannot be made with this limitation (with some exceptions). I will admit, there may be some parts of this project where I "cheated" and used 3D prints instead of a complicated LEGO setup, but due to a lack of time, I had to make compromises.

[^1]: Calculated by taking the number of LEGO components and dividing by the total number of components reported in the BOM within the SolidWorks drawings

[^2]: I defined classical LEGO as the bricks and parts that interface with them (i.e., no Technic)

## Cost

The cost for LEGO will vary depending on local availability. The costs in the BOM reflect what I ended up needing to purchase. Having been fortunate enough to be raised with lots of LEGO, I only needed a handful of parts. However, even without the parts, I believe you could build this printer without breaking the bank.
